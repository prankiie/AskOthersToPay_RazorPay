Executive summary · 1 of 2

Asking someone to pay is common. It's never had a clean digital path.

A daughter at Croma. A spouse at a restaurant. An accountant forwarding an invoice. It happens constantly — offline and informally.

Slide 1
Ask Others to Pay

The need is persistent

  • Priya on Croma checkout — UPI has failed twice; asks her father.
  • Meera at The Bombay Canteen — scans the bill QR, asks her husband.
  • Anita (accountant) forwards a Razorpay Payment Link to her CFO.
  • Today this resolves as a phone call followed by a separate UPI transfer — off Razorpay's rails, no order linkage, no audit trail.

No existing primitive fits

  • UPI P2P collect came closest — banned by NPCI Oct 2025 after widespread fraud abuse.
  • UPI Circle addresses a narrow slice — pre-registered trusted users with spending limits. Requires setup, no merchant context, not designed for ad-hoc delegation at the payment moment.
  • Everything else: phone call + manual transfer. No merchant context, no trust signals, no audit trail, no receipt.
Why Razorpay: this fits Razorpay's existing surfaces (Checkout, POS QR, Payment Link), brings off-rail volume on-rail, and — because delegated payments emit the same webhooks as direct ones — reaches merchants' backends without integration work.
→
The open question is adoption, not whether the need exists. Only real-world usage can answer it — engineering alone can't.

Unique index: one_open_delegation_per_order. A second open delegation on the same order returns 409 ORDER_HAS_OPEN_DELEGATION.
Tier is never user-supplied. Derived from merchant KYC, MCC, tenure, and risk flags in assignTrustTier(). The client cannot coerce it.
TTL is a request-validity window, not a payment SLA. It governs how long the approver has to act — 24 h for web, 15 min for POS, inherits the Payment Link's own expiry.

→

Architecture · 5 of 5

Webhook contract

Four new delegation events. The payment result fires the same two events as direct payments — which is why merchants need no integration work.

Slide 12
Ask Others to Pay
New delegation lifecycle events
order.delegation_requested Fires on delegation create. Payload carries delegation.id, status: PENDING, order.id.
Lets the merchant reserve inventory or mark the order as awaiting a third-party approval.
order.delegation_approved Fires when the approver submits payment and Razorpay authorizes it. Precedes payment.captured.
Confirms the delegation resolved positively. Optional for merchants.
order.delegation_declined Fires when the approver declines. Order reverts to created.
Merchant can release held inventory or notify the buyer.
order.delegation_expired Fires when TTL elapses without an approver decision. Order reverts to created.
Symmetrical to declined from the merchant's point of view.
Standard payment events — identical to direct payments
payment.captured Standard Razorpay event. Payload is identical to direct-payment capture except notes carries delegation_id for optional reconciliation.
Merchant's existing capture handler fires unchanged.
order.paid Standard Razorpay event. Order transitions to paid exactly as in a direct payment.
This is what fulfillment keys off — no handler change needed.
→
The last two events are the adoption property. A merchant who never registers the new delegation events still receives the payment as a normal capture. Delegation awareness is additive, not required.

Integrity · what makes this safe to ship

Fraud & abuse mitigations

The primary risk vector is a malicious requestor using the preamble to impersonate Razorpay or misdirect the approver. Controls are layered at sanitization, rendering, and platform levels.

Slide 16
Ask Others to Pay

Anti-spoofing on the outgoing message

  • Server-side sanitization — URLs, phone-number-shaped strings, Unicode bidi overrides (U+202A–E, U+2066–9), and homoglyphs of "Razorpay" all stripped before concatenation.
  • Hard separator — two newlines between preamble and system block. System block always starts with the literal header.
  • URL locking — exactly one URL allowed in the outgoing message: the canonical rzp.io/r/{id}. Anything else is removed.
  • Length cap — preamble hard-capped at 140 chars; requests over return HTTP 400. Full message engineered to fit 2 GSM-7 segments.
  • DLT template gating — SMS sends go through pre-registered DLT templates; tampering is rejected at carrier ingress.

Primitive-level integrity

  • One open delegation per order — DB-level unique constraint. Second attempt returns 409 ORDER_HAS_OPEN_DELEGATION.
  • Server-owned tier assignment — tier comes from Razorpay's merchant data pipeline; client cannot coerce it.
  • Eligibility gated upstream — merchants with tenure < 30 days or high risk flag can't initiate at all; the button doesn't render.
  • System block regenerated on every open — no trust via link inspection; the approval page re-derives from fresh merchant data.
  • No requestor-name line on the approval page — identity rides the delivery channel; Razorpay does not fabricate one.
  • Phased by trust tier — proposed v1 rollout is to Razorpay-selected merchants only; biometric step-up and CAPTCHA are deferred scale-up levers, not v1 requirements.
→
None of these controls rely on user vigilance. They're structural — the preamble literally cannot reproduce the system block format, and a second delegation on the same order literally cannot be created.

Legal & regulatory (parallel track)

  • Chargeback T&C — liability allocation between approver and merchant.
  • DPDP / DPO sign-off — data classification and retention.
  • Approval page wording — RBI consumer-protection review.

Open empirical questions

  • Approval rate — what % of shared delegations get approved?
  • Completion rate — what % of approvals result in captured payments?
  • Time to resolution — share to paid, end-to-end.
